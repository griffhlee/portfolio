Top 10

gave this some part more thought.

10. Time After Time - Chet Baker

"I only know what I know"

The song to walk around a city at night to.

This whole album is a masterpiece, but this is the standout for me.

I first listened to this when I was in Paris for my semester abroad, and it is what drew me into the city. I remember listening to this on repeat as I walked around the city at night. It brings back great memories. Chet's got a great voice too, one that cuts right through.

9. Hero - Family of the Year

"Let me go"

A song to make you feel change.

Check out my Boyhood (link) entry and you can see why I like this song.

This song has been a constant for me over the years. It is one I'll play during big moments of change to remind myself of my own boyhood.

8. Fade Into You - Mazzy Star

"Color your eyes with what's not there"

My favorite love song.

This is just a great moody tune that can make you feel real deep. A lot of Mazzy Star songs can do that, but this one especially. Really great lyrics too.

7. Heroes - David Bowie

"We can be Heroes / Just for one day"

A song that will make you feel infinite.

Such a powerful song. It is an anthem. I just realized I have two songs named Hero(es) on this list. Anyways, this is a great song to drive and sing along to.

6. Piledriver Waltz - Alex Turner

"Mysteries flashing amber"

Alex Turner movie soundtrack genius.

Definitely a newer addition to the list but I can't stop listening to it. The album version is great, but the Submarine soundtrack version is even better.

Something about it, not really sure how to describe it. Doesn't give you much time to breathe. Just a solid 3 some minutes of picture painting.

5. California Stars - Billy Bragg, Wilco

"I'd love to feel your hand touching mine"

God bless Woody Guthrie.

Billy Bragg and Wilco made this album re-doing Woody lyrics. They stumbled upon some old song lyrics he hadn't recorded which became California Stars. This is a great one to put on in the kitchen as you cook at night.

4. Amsterdam - Gregory Alan Isakov

"That howling wind, she'll take everything"

Hit the road and don't look back.

I'm not sure what switched or happened over the past couple years, but I have not stopped listening to him.

You saw a couple of his songs on this list, and Amsterdam was the catalyst. Always makes me think of heading west.

Great song for a rainy day, too.

3. This Must Be the Place (Naive Melody) - Talking Heads

"Home is where I want to be"

Super goofy instant dance song madness.

Now this is a fun song. Fun to dance to and fun to sing along. Big fan of the Stop Making Sense version as well.

He wears that damn silly suit and it gets me everytime.

2. Kids - MGMT

"Control yourself"

The ultimate indie song.

I listened this throughout high school, college, and now post-grad. Can feel the riff in the veins.

A great song to put on at a party. Another one I'll dance to and sing along to on auto.

The Indie Rock revolution of the early 2000s and 2010s has a grip on me and this is one that won't let go.

1. New Slang - The Shins

"When you notice the stripes"

The perfect song.

I remember randomly finding this song back in the fall of 2019. I added it to my "fall" playlist, not thinking too much about it.

It's been 6 years now of listening to New Slang and this was the easiest pick of them all.

From 0:00 to end it hits every mark. A song that'll change the direction of your life when you hear it. Maybe I'm hyping it up too much, but you should listen to it if you haven't.

Hope you enjoyed the list. Sorry to the songs that didn't make it. Maybe next year is your year.
