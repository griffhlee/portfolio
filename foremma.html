For Emma, Forever Ago - Bon Iver

Journal Entry - 11.03.2024

"Bags" by Clairo, released in 2019 as part of her debut album Immunity, is a deeply emotional and introspective track that explores the complexity of unspoken feelings and the vulnerability of navigating an uncertain relationship. Combining lo-fi aesthetics with a gentle indie rock sound, "Bags" marks a significant departure from Clairo's earlier bedroom pop style, showcasing a more mature and nuanced approach to songwriting and production.

The Lyrics: A Study of Emotional Ambiguity

At its core, "Bags" is about the hesitation and confusion that comes with realizing deep feelings for someone while being uncertain about how those feelings will be received. The song is filled with subtle yet poignant expressions of emotional vulnerability. From the opening line, “Every second counts, I don’t want to talk to you anymore,” Clairo establishes a sense of urgency and discomfort, capturing the tension of wanting to express feelings but being paralyzed by the fear of rejection.

Throughout the song, Clairo juxtaposes moments of openness with a reluctance to fully confront her emotions, which adds to the song’s sense of emotional ambiguity. Lines like “Can you see me? I’m waiting for the right time” and “It’s always just that same old punchline” hint at the frustration and longing that characterize the narrator's experience. She is waiting for a perfect moment that never seems to come, caught between her desire to be vulnerable and the fear of making herself too exposed.

The lyric "Walking out the door with your bags" is a metaphor for departure, signaling a relationship or situation that is unresolved and possibly ending. It suggests that the subject of the song is leaving, both literally and emotionally, which underscores the theme of unreciprocated feelings. The use of the word "bags" suggests the weight of emotional baggage, not just the physical act of leaving, but also carrying unresolved feelings with you.

The Sound: Minimalism with Impact

Musically, "Bags" is understated yet powerful, blending soft, minimal instrumentation with atmospheric production. The song features a simple guitar riff that repeats throughout, acting as a backdrop for Clairo’s delicate vocals. This minimalist approach complements the intimate nature of the lyrics, allowing the emotional depth of the song to shine through without overwhelming the listener.

One of the standout elements of "Bags" is its use of space and quietness. The production, handled by Clairo and Rostam Batmanglij, allows for moments of stillness that emphasize the emotional weight of each line. The song doesn't build to a dramatic climax, but rather maintains a steady, contemplative pace, mirroring the internal struggle of the narrator. This restraint in both production and vocal delivery enhances the song's introspective nature.

Themes of Identity and Queerness

"Bags" has also been interpreted as a reflection on Clairo’s experience of coming to terms with her sexuality. The song subtly addresses the complexities of queer relationships and the particular struggles that can come with expressing romantic feelings in that context. While the song doesn't explicitly reference Clairo’s queerness, the ambiguity of the lyrics leaves room for multiple interpretations, making it relatable to a broad audience while also resonating with those who have had similar experiences of navigating same-sex relationships.
